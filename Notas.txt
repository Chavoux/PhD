Shady bet? Dempster-Shafer theory tells you that since there is no evidence either way, you should have Bel(Heads) = 0 and Bel(¬Heads) = 0. But if an expert says she is 90% certain that the coin is fair, your beliefs are different with Bel(Heads) = 0.9 × 0.5 = 0.45 and also Bel(¬Heads) = 0.9 × 0.5 = 0.45.  The expert evidence has reduced the probability interval for Heads from [0, 1] to [0.45, 0.55]. Bel(Heads) = 0.45, maar Plausibility(Heads) = 0.55 = 1-Bel(¬Heads) (= 1 - Bel(Tails) in hierdie geval.
